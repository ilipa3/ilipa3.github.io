Abstract




Indonesia is one of the most polluted countries in the world. Air pollution in Indonesia especially in Jakarta can be caused by some unsavory human behavior. For instance, burning garbage, forests that are deliberately set on fire by humans, excessive use of electricity which means burning coal must be carried out on a very large scale, even up to 58 million tons a year. Air pollution caused by human activities can have an impact on global warming. Global warming itself is a phenomenon where the earth's average temperature rises due to increased amounts of greenhouse gasses. Therefore, in an effort to reduce global warming, humans are starting to look for other energy, namely alternative energy sources. Alternative energy can be in the form of solar power, hydro power, and wind power. Now Indonesia is starting the development of wind power as a power plant. Indonesia itself has the potential for wind power plants of 60.6 GW (Giga Watt), however, currently only a small amount of potential has been utilized by the government. In this research, we made mock-ups of eco-friendly housing where we use wind as a power generator. The mockup will be given an artificial wind that can rotate the blades of the windmill. Then the generator behind the propeller converts the mechanical energy into electrical energy. We hope that with this research, Indonesia can further develop its wind turbines.
